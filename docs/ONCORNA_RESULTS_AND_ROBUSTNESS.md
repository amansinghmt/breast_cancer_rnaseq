- **Verified from files:** read directly from canonical TSVs, logs or manifests.

## PCA sensitivity

The revised PCA uses DESeq2 VST (`blind=FALSE`) rather than the previous log2-CPM fallback. PC1/PC2 now explain approximately 32.5%/15.1%, compared with 30.4%/14.3% in the archived log2-CPM figure. This is a presentation transformation change, not a change to the DE model. No sample exceeds the predefined robust PC1-PC2 distance threshold of 3.5. This does not prove that no sample influences higher components or that no batch effect exists.

## Methodological changes

- Primary DE model, contrast, shrinkage method and reporting thresholds: unchanged.
- Added explicit statements of existing DESeq2 defaults while preserving `alpha=0.1` filter optimization.
- Added VST output for exploratory figures only.
- GO output now stores all tested terms; significant count unchanged.
- Added a semantic-reduction presentation table.
- Added one predefined low-count sensitivity rerun; it does not replace canonical results.

## Unresolved issues

- No external matched cohort replication.
- No clinical covariates beyond available metadata are modeled.
- No cell-composition deconvolution or batch-sensitivity model is included.
- Identifier mapping loss may bias pathway coverage.
- The biological reason for Normal-higher genes and negative NES pathways requires tissue-context expertise and validation.
- `normal` LFC shrinkage is preserved for reproducibility although alternatives may reduce bias.

## Exact limits of inference

The project supports reproducible statistical association within this cohort and pathway-level hypotheses. It cannot support causation, diagnosis, prognosis, treatment response, biomarker validation, patient-level prediction or generalization to all breast cancers.
